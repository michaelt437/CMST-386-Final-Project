Windows 10 event scheduled Jan. 21st

The next iteration of everyone's favorite operating system for PC's is on the horizon. Microsoft has scheduled a special press conference for January 21st in Redmond, Washington that will focus on new features of Windows 10. For us at home, there will be a livestream.

This conference is aimed at detailing more "consumer-features", which may include ideas on more touch and multitouch functionalities, as well as features surrounding the possible integration with Windows phones, tablets, and the Xbox One.

In a move that can be viewed by some as strange, Microsoft has announced earlier this year that they will skip Windows 9 altogether and release Windows 10 as their next big update to their flagship operating system. Why the number 9 is getting the cold shoulder, we will never truly know.

Aside from possible indications of superficial fears of numbers, at least it tells us that Microsoft is moving forward. Windows 8 seemed to have gained back the love lost during the Vista/7 era, and from the early looks of Windows 10, people who have gotten used to the current version will not have to re-learn how to navigate their own computer.
